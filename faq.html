Frequently Asked Questions

A mutual fund is a professionally-managed investment scheme, usually run by an asset management company that brings together a group of people and invests their money in stocks, bonds and other securities.

Key benefits of investing in Mutual Funds are:

• Professional Management: Many investors do not have the time or expertise to manage their personal investments on regular basis. Mutual funds are managed by professionals who are experienced in investing money and who have the education, skills and resources to research investment opportunities to generate superior risk adjusted return.

• Access to IPO: Usually, an IPO share returns tremendously at the first trading day in the market. General investors are allotted maximum 50.0% of IPO shares. But, an individual investor can invest a limited amount (around BDT 5,000) in a single IPO. On the other hand, IPO shares that are allotted for general investors are usually remain highly oversubscribed. Hence, general public have a very lower probability of having the allotment. However, Mutual funds have the certainty to have 10.0% of IPO shares. As a result, general investors who subscribe in the mutual fund can attain the certain access to IPO allocation with a much high amount. So, general investors’ upside potentiality widens.

• Diversification: A single mutual fund can hold securities from hundreds or even thousands of issuers that a single investor can’t do. This diversification considerably reduces the risk of a serious monetary loss due to problems in a particular company or industry.

• Affordability: Investors can buy units or shares with a relatively small amount of money. Some mutual funds also offer Systemic Investment Plan (SIP) to facilitate investment on a regular basis with even smaller installments.

• Liquidity: Units or shares of a mutual fund can be bought and sold in any business day at NAV applicable on that respective date, providing investors easy access to their money.

• Transparency: As per regulatory requirement, the financial details must be disclosed to regulatory bodies. Mutual Fund is one of the most strictly regulated investment vehicles. Laws governing our fund require exhaustive disclosure to the BSEC as well as to the unit holders. Fund will be audited yearly by a licensed auditor. In addition to that, investors’ assets are kept in the custody of leading licensed custodian, ensuring the best security of the fund. Moreover, we will issue electronic shares in favor of investors. Therefore, investors can easily check their holdings through Central Depository of Bangladesh Limited (CDBL).

• Tax Benefit: The investment tax benefits of the Fund would always comply with country’s Finance Act and government’s tax policies. However, currently the following benefits are available:

Tax Advantage on Income: Dividend income from a mutual fund or a unit fund up to BDT 25,000.00 (Twenty-five Thousand Taka only) is exempted from tax as per clause 22A of 6th Schedule (Part A) under Income Tax Ordinance, 1984. The capital gain made from selling of our funds are completely tax exempted.

Investment Tax Credit: According to the Section 44(2)(c) under Income Tax Ordinance, 1984, the amount of allowable investment is actual investment or 25.0% of total taxable income or BDT 1,50,00,000.00 (Taka One crore & Fifty Lac only), whichever is lower.

Example: an individual’s total taxable income is BDT 3,600,000 in a year and actual investment is BDT 1,500,000. His eligible for tax rebate will be lowest amount of the following figures:

Actual Investment
 BDT 1,500,000
25.0% of total taxable income
 BDT 900,000 (25.0% x BDT 36,00,000)
Maximum eligible investment
 BDT 15,000,000

Considering the above three figures, the eligible investment will be BDT 900,000 only. As his total taxable income is above BDT 3,000,000 so the total tax rebate for the individual will be calculated as follows:

a) 1st BDT 250,000 of eligible amount at the rate of 15%
 BDT 37,500 (15% x BDT 2,50,000)
b) Next BDT 500,000 of eligible amount at the rate of 12%
 BDT 60,000 (12% x BDT 5,00,000)
c) Remaining of eligible amount i.e. next BDT 150,000 at the rate of 10%  
 BDT 15,000 (10% x BDT 1,50,000)
Total Taxable rebate
 BDT 112,500

The investor can reduce tax liability of BDT 112,500 by investing in this fund.

All investments by funds are tax exempted (e.g., Interest income from Fixed Deposit Receipt (FDR), Dividend income, Capital gain) which helps to generate superior return to the investor.
